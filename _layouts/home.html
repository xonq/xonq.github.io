I specialize in discovering genes that underlie traits-of-interest by intergrating genomics with other -omic data. My overall expertise is in mass spectrometry, evolutionary biology, molecular ecology, and multi-omic analysis. I provide consultation in bioinformatics/computational biology and mycology (fungal/mushroom) education.

My training includes 5 years in natural product drug discovery and quality control analytical chemistry (industry and academic), followed by 5 years in computational genomics focusing on detection and evolution of fungal small molecule biosynthesis. My work in Jason Slot's lab culminated in 1) an evolution-informed natural product biosynthesis targeting platform that can recover biosynthetic pathways with increased accuracy and recovery compared to published standards in the field; and 2) a platform for automating large-scale comparative phylogenomics. I am driven to address applied questions in industry, where I have helped identify flavor gene candidates in shiitake mushrooms by integrating transcriptomic and phylogenomic data. I employed another tailored genome-guided strategy to pinpoint clusters of colocalized genes that produce analogs of the psychoactive drug, LSD.

I received my PhD in Dr. Jason Slot's lab at The Ohio State University as an Ohio Agricultural Research and Development Center Associate. Prior to my PhD, I worked in industry as a quality control chemist, implementing chromatography and mass spectrometry. I was trained in mass spectrometry and chromatography as a natural product drug discovery chemist in Dr. Sandra Loesgen's lab for four years.
