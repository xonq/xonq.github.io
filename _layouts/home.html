My work is primarily at the confluence of evolutionary theory and genomic analysism, with a speciality in computationally identifying metabolic pathways leveraging multi-omic data. I began as an analytical chemist, and synthesized my passion for evolutionary theory and drug discovery by transitioning into computational genomics. My dissertation in Jason Slot's lab culminated in 1) an evolution-informed natural product biosynthesis targeting platform that can identify non-canonical metabolic pathways with greater accuracy and recovery compared to published standards in the field; and 2) a platform for automating large-scale comparative phylogenomics. I am driven to address applied questions in industry, where I have helped identify flavor gene candidates in shiitake mushrooms by integrating transcriptomic and phylogenomic data. I extended this work pinpointed clusters of colocalized genes that produce analogs of the psychoactive drug, LSD. I am open to employment, contracting, and consultation - please follow and contact me.

I received my PhD in Dr. Jason Slot's lab at The Ohio State University Ohio Agricultural Research and Development Center Associate. Prior to my PhD, I worked in industry as a quality control chemist, implementing chromatography and mass spectrometry. I was trained in mass spectrometry and chromatography as a natural product drug discovery chemist in Dr. Sandra Loesgen's lab for four years.
