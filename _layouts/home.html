I am a versatile computational biologist, genome scientist, mycologist, and analytical chemist. My focus lies in large-scale genomics analysis and automation, specificially within the field of metabologenomic-driven drug discovery. I possess a broad skillset that is generally applicable to multiomic analysis. I have successfully implemented this knowledge-base in automation software that has provided valuable insights into multiple applied systems. For instance, I have integrated transcriptomic and phylogenomic analysis to identify industrially-important flavor genes in shiitake mushrooms. Additionally, I pinpointed clusters of colocalized genes that produce analogs of the psychoactive drug, LSD. I scaled this approach by generalizing gene cluster detection to elucidate the genetic underpinnings of microbial natural product synthesis with unprecendented recovery and output quality. My expertise across these disciplines uniquely qualifies me to provide valuable insights into diverse systems and automate complex multiomic analysis.

I am currently finalizing my PhD with Dr. Jason Slot at The Ohio State University (August 11th), where I am a Translational Plant Sciences Fellow, Ohio Agricultural Research and Development Center Associate, and University Fellow. I previously worked in industry as a quality control chemist, implementing chromatography and mass spectrometry to quantify terpenoid, pesticide, and residual solvent concentrations. I was trained in mass spectrometry and chromatography as a natural product drug discovery chemist in Dr. Sandra Loesgen's lab for four years.
