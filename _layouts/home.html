I am a computational genomics contractor and consultant. My expertise spans a spectrum across computational biology, genomics, fungal biology, and analytical chemistry. My focus lies in large-scale genomics analysis and automation, specificially within the field of metabologenomic-driven drug discovery. I possess a broad skillset that is generally applicable to multiomic analysis and has provided valuable insights into multiple applied systems. Examples of my contributions include: identifying industrially-important flavor genes in shiitake mushrooms by integrating transcriptomic and phylogenomic data; pinpointing clusters of colocalized genes that produce analogs of the psychoactive drug, LSD; and developing a bleeding-edge genomic-driven natural product biosynthesis targeting platform that greatly exceeds current industry standards. My expertise across these disciplines uniquely qualifies me to provide valuable insight in genomic-driven drug discovery research and computational biology in general.

I received my PhD in Dr. Jason Slot's lab at The Ohio State University as a Translational Plant Sciences Fellow, Ohio Agricultural Research and Development Center Associate, and University Fellow. Prior to my PhD, I worked in industry as a quality control chemist, implementing chromatography and mass spectrometry to quantify terpenoid, pesticide, and residual solvent concentrations. I was trained in mass spectrometry and chromatography as a natural product drug discovery chemist in Dr. Sandra Loesgen's lab for four years.
