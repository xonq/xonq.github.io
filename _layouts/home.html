I am a computational biologist, genome scientist, mycologist, and analytical chemist. I specialize in large-scale genomics analysis and automation in the context of metabologenomic drug discovery, though my expertise is generally applicable to multiomic analysis. I have deployed the automation software I developed to present insight into applied systems, such as: identifying industrially-important flavor genes in shiitake mushrooms by synthesizing transcriptomic and phylogenomic analysis; pinpointing bioactive gene clusters that produce analogs of the psychoactive drug, LSD; and engineering a generalized gene cluster detection framework that identifies natural product-yielding gene clusters with unprecendented recovery and and output quality. My expertise across computer science, evolutionary theory, and analytical chemistry uniquely positions me to present insight into diverse systems and automate routine-complex multi-omic analysis.

I am currently finalizing my PhD with Dr. Jason Slot at The Ohio State University (August 11th), where I am a Translational Plant Sciences Fellow, Ohio Agricultural Research and Development Center Associate, and University Fellow. I previously worked in industry as a quality control chemist, implementing chromatography and mass spectrometry to quantify terpenoid, pesticide, and residual solvent concentrations. I was trained in mass spectrometry and chromatography as a natural product drug discovery chemist in Dr. Sandra Loesgen's lab for four years.
