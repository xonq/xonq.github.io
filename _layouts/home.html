I have 10 years of experience across the continuum of metabologenomic-driven drug discovery and large-scale genomics analysis and automation. My time in analytical chemistry and genome biology research cultivated a skillset that spans applied evolutionary theory, genome science, fungal biology, bioinformatics, and analytical chemistry. Examples of the applied contributions of my work include: identifying industrially-important flavor genes in shiitake mushrooms by integrating transcriptomic and phylogenomic data; pinpointing clusters of colocalized genes that produce analogs of the psychoactive drug, LSD; and developing a genome-driven natural product biosynthesis targeting platform that greatly exceeds current published standards. Throughout my time in drug discovery, I have become familiar of the challenges of the old "needle in the haystack" approach of redundantly investigating lineages for novel compounds, and am inspired to further my work to increase the throughput of identifying new compounds; deconvolute gene cluster heterologous expression; and bring cryptic lineages with high potential for novel discovery into the fold. I am open to employment, contracting, and consultation - please follow and contact me.

I received my PhD in Dr. Jason Slot's lab at The Ohio State University as a Translational Plant Sciences Fellow, Ohio Agricultural Research and Development Center Associate, and University Fellow. Prior to my PhD, I worked in industry as a quality control chemist, implementing chromatography and mass spectrometry to quantify terpenoid, pesticide, and residual solvent concentrations. I was trained in mass spectrometry and chromatography as a natural product drug discovery chemist in Dr. Sandra Loesgen's lab for four years.
