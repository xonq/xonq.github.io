I am a computational biologist focused on genome science with 10 years experience across chemistry and bioinformatics disciplines. I specialize in discovering and optimizing genetic pathways underlying biological traits-of-interest. To this end, I employ a variety of tailored multi-omic analyses that include general next-generation sequencing (NGS) analysis, mass spectrometry, phylogenomics, RNAseq, and genomic discovery. My experience has been applied to eukaryotes (fungi and plants) as well as prokaryotes, and includes invited teaching and workshop presentations for technical expertise and general botany/mycology education.

My career includes both industry and academic experience, with 5 years in natural product drug discovery analytical chemistry, followed by 5 years in computational genomics focusing on identifying underlying genes and optimizing/engineering expression of phenotypes-of-interest. I currently work as a post-doctoral researcher and industry scientific consultant focused on plant pathogenic bacteria and cryptic fungal biosynthetic pathways. My PhD in Jason Slot's lab culminated in 1) an evolution-informed natural product biosynthesis targeting platform that can recover biosynthetic pathways with increased accuracy and recovery compared to published standards in the field; and 2) a platform for automating large-scale comparative phylogenomics. I am driven to address applied questions in industry, where I have helped identify flavor gene candidates in shiitake mushrooms by integrating transcriptomic and phylogenomic data. I employed another tailored genome-guided strategy to pinpoint clusters of colocalized genes that produce analogs of the psychoactive drug, LSD.
