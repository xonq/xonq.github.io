I am a highly collaborative computational biologist passionate about addressing biological research questions at the frontier of -omics analysis. I emphasize revealing biological insight by conceptually understanding and modeling systems with multi-omics data, though I am enthusiastic to address recalcitrant questions via machine learning. My methodological background is centered on NGS data analysis, big data genomics, RNAseq, mass spectrometry, phylogenomics, and discovering/optimizing genetic pathways underlying phenotypes-of-interest. My interests have been applied to eukaryotes (fungi and plants) as well as prokaryotes, and include invited teaching and workshop presentations for technical expertise and general botany/mycology education.

My career includes both industry and academic experience, with 5 years in natural product drug discovery analytical chemistry, followed by 6 years in computational genomics focusing on identifying underlying genes and optimizing/engineering expression of phenotypes-of-interest. I currently work as a post-doctoral researcher and industry scientific consultant focused on plant pathogenic bacteria and cryptic fungal biosynthetic pathways. My PhD in Jason Slot's lab culminated in 1) an evolution-informed natural product biosynthesis targeting platform that can recover biosynthetic pathways with increased accuracy and recovery compared to published standards in the field; and 2) a platform for automating large-scale comparative phylogenomics. I am driven to address applied questions in industry, where I have helped identify flavor gene candidates in shiitake mushrooms by integrating transcriptomic and phylogenomic data. I employed another tailored genome-guided strategy to pinpoint clusters of colocalized genes that produce analogs of the psychoactive drug, LSD.
