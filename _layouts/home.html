I am fascinated by evolutionary theory and how following its trail can lead to the discovery of natural products.

I have 10 years of experience across the continuum of metabologenomic-driven drug discovery and large-scale genomics analysis and automation. I became familiar with the challenges of genome-guided natural product discovery as an analytical chemist, and synthesized my passion for evolutionary theory and drug discovery by transitioning into computational genomics research. The pinnacle community contributions of my work are 1) an evolution-informed natural product biosynthesis targeting platform that more accurately and comprehensively recovers gene clusters compared to the published standards in the field; and 2) a platform for automating large-scale comparative phylogenomics. I am driven to leverage my software and skillset to address applied questions in industry, such as identifying industrially-important flavor genes in shiitake mushrooms by integrating transcriptomic and phylogenomic data and pinpointing clusters of colocalized genes that produce analogs of the psychoactive drug, LSD. I am open to employment, contracting, and consultation - please follow and contact me.

I received my PhD in Dr. Jason Slot's lab at The Ohio State University as a Translational Plant Sciences Fellow, Ohio Agricultural Research and Development Center Associate, and University Fellow. Prior to my PhD, I worked in industry as a quality control chemist, implementing chromatography and mass spectrometry to quantify terpenoid, pesticide, and residual solvent concentrations. I was trained in mass spectrometry and chromatography as a natural product drug discovery chemist in Dr. Sandra Loesgen's lab for four years.
